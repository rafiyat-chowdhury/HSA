    Sanda, also known as Sanshou or Chinese kickboxing, is a Chinese martial art and combat sport that combines techniques from traditional Chinese martial arts, modern combat sports, and military hand-to-hand combat.

    Sanda involves striking techniques such as punches, kicks, and knee strikes, as well as wrestling, throwing, joint locks, clinching and grappling techniques. It also incorporates the use of protective gear, including gloves and headgear, and follows rules and regulations for safety during competition.

    Sanda training can also help develop important skills for self-defense, such as situational awareness, confidence, and mental resilience.

    Sanda in MMA

    Sanda is a popular combat sport in China, and is also practiced in other parts of the world as a form of self-defense or for fitness and exercise.

    There have been many notable Sanda fighters throughout history. Here are a few:

    1. Cung Le: martial artist who has competed in both Sanda and mixed martial arts (MMA). He is a former Strikeforce Middleweight Champion and has also acted in movies and television shows.
    2. Li Jingliang - a Chinese fighter who has a background in Sanda and is known for his powerful striking. He has fought in the UFC since 2014.
    3. Muslim Salikhov - a Russian fighter who is a former Sanda and kickboxing champion. He has competed in the UFC since 2017
